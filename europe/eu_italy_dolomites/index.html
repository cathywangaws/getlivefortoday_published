God’s Back Garden – the Dolomites, Italy

2019.12.05

God’s Back Garden – the Dolomites, Italy

The Dolomites in the North of Italy boast some of the mightiest granite peaks of Europe. This region is home to a spectacular road that provides both exhilarating and scenic driving.

The breathtaking region around the Dolomites of Italy is one of the most beautiful places on earth. We visited it in October which the fall color was changing that made our road trip more romantic.

The picturesque countryside is full of rugged mountains, scenic hikes, beautiful lakes with brilliant turquoise waters, and charming, tiny villages straight out of a fairytale.

The mountainous trail boasts a charming contrast between scenic valleys and dramatic vertical peaks, each presenting its own array of natural beauty.

Hiking in autumn in the Dolomites is otherworldly. The larch tree needles turn golden yellow, the air is crisp and fresh, and there are fewer tourists to share the trail with!

Walk peaceful paths through ancient villages and imagine life here in the past and its intersection with the present…

Most importantly, we enjoyed ourselves! The Dolomites is arguably one of the most beautiful and captivating places on Earth. The weather was awesome during our visiting in the Dolomites. Local people were so friendly to us. Breathtaking trails elevated our spirits. Small villages allowed us to take a step back in time and experienced the simpler things in life. There are sights here that no camera can capture, so we took our time and took it all in. This trip in year 2018 left us with memories to last a lifetime!

The North of Italy - Driving the Dolomites, Italy

First, we stopped at a visitor center at Longarone, 32013 Province of Belluno, Italy. The visitor center has nobody except one guy sit behind the counter. He gave us very good information about Dolomites area and he told us we were lucky since the weather in Dolomites was great this week. Usually, mountain areas have lot of rain and cloudy sky!

The Dolomites in the North of Italy have some of the granite peaks of Europe. This region is home to a spectacular road that provides both exhilarating and scenic driving. The Great Dolomites Road takes you from Bolzano to Cortina d’Ampezzo through the heart of the UNESCO-listed Dolomites.

As we climbed up attractive mountain landscapes open up to us and the unique saw-tooth peaks appear. The ‘Grande strada delle Dolomiti’ encompasses the iconic grey peaks and plateaus but also forested hills and green valleys. The combination of these two colors create a spectacular contrasts. From the 2239 meters peak The Great Dolomites Road snakes down along a magnificent valley that includes a lake (Lago di Carezza), a castle (Andraz castle) and a cable car that provides excellent views of the surrounding nature.
