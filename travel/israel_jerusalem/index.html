An ancient city where every stone has a story to tell - Jerusalem, Israel

2013.11.24

Life is about making memories. I had an unforgettable solo trip to Jerusalem, Israel a couple of years ago. As soon as I stood at the Jaffa Gate of this ancient walled city, I was speechless. More than beautiful, Jerusalem is a spiritual center, holy to the three religions. That three religions have assigned so much importance to a solitary city makes Jerusalem one of the most fascinating places I have ever visited.

Jerusalem is a city suspended between heaven and earth, East and West, past and present. For some people, Jerusalem is a condition, like being in love; for others, it is a state of mind, a constant tension between rival flags and faiths. The word “unique” is easy to throw around, but Jerusalem has a real claim on it.

Christians quietly praying in the Church of the Holy Sepulchre, Jews dancing by the Western Wall and Muslims prostrate before Al-Aqsa Mosque. It is hard not to be swept up in the emotional tide that tremble through these ancient streets.

There were many things I wanted to do in Jerusalem, and walking the Via Dolorosa 苦路, Way of the Sorrows, was high on my list. The route is about the path that Jesus walked, carrying his cross, on the way to his crucifixion. I am not necessarily a religious person, but I had read the Bible and I wanted to see the 14 Stations of the Cross.

After I completed the Via Dolorosa and visited all fourteen stations of the cross, I wasn’t sure what to do next. I flitted about the Church for a few minutes before deciding that I really couldn’t concentrate, and found a nearby café where I enjoyed fresh pomegranate juice and thought about my day of historical pilgrimage. Whatever motivations for visiting Jerusalem and exploring its past, the walk along the pilgrimage path so important to Christians for centuries is a unique way to connect to the storied past of this unusual but endlessly fascinating city.
