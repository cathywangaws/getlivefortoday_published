Salted Duck

Nanjing Salted Duck has over 2000 years of history. It looks plain, but is packed with flavor. You’ll need:

one whole 4 – 4 ½ pound duck
4 tablespoons salt (1/3 cup)
2 ½ tablespoons Sichuan peppercorns (half 1/3 cup)
4 star anise
3 bay leaves
1 cinnamon stick
5 slices ginger
3 scallions, cut into large segments
1/2 cup Shaoxing wine

Directions:

Rinse the duck thoroughly under cold water, discarding any of the giblets that came with it. Thoroughly pat it dry with paper towels, inside and out.

In the meantime, add the salt and Sichuan peppercorns to a flat-bottomed pan over medium heat. Dry roast them by stirring constantly for 5-8 minutes until the salt turns slightly yellow. Turn off the heat and let cool. Be sure not to burn the Sichuan peppercorns.

Rub the salt and Sichuan peppercorns all over the duck, inside and out. Use all of the mixture, making sure that you use at least 1⁄3 for the cavity. Let the duck marinate, uncovered, for three hours in the refrigerator or for overnight.

While the duck is marinating, you can prepare your spice pouch. Place the star anise, bay leaves, cinnamon, and peppercorns on a square of cheesecloth and tie tightly with kitchen twine. Set aside.

Next, prepare a pot that is big enough to cook the duck whole. Fill it with 12-15 cups of water, enough to submerge the whole duck. Add the spice pouch along with the ginger and scallion. Bring the pot of water to a boil, and add the Shaoxing wine.

Broil water for about 20 minutes and then add the duck into the pot with medium heat for 30 minutes. Turn off the heat but don’t open the lid. Let it stay for another hour.

Cut into small pieces to serve.

Green Onion Oil Recipe

Ingredients

1 big bunch of green onions finely sliced
3 tablespoons of ginger finely minced
1/4 cup vegetable oil. Do not use olive oil.

Note: Olive oil has a very strong flavour which doesn’t do well in this sauce and masks the flavors of it.

Directions

Heat up the oil in a saucepan over high heat until the oil is shimmery and hot, but not smoking. Add the green onions, ginger at once, but be careful, the oil will bubble and splatter. The onions will sizzle and wilt almost immediately and turn a bright green. Take the pan off the heat and stir the sauce with a wooden spoon.
