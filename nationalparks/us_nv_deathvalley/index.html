#Land of Extremes

Death Valley National Park can be summed up in one word: extreme. Because it is the hottest, lowest, and driest place in the United States.

The record high temperature was 134F on July 10, 1913. Death Valley’s hottest “unoffical” temperature was 201F measured on the ground in direct sunlight. By contrast, winter nights can be quiet cool. The colderst temperature recorded at Furnace Creek Ranch was 15F in 1913.

Badwater Basin is the lowest spot in the Western Hemisphere. Teh exact lowest spot is 282 feet below sea levels, but it is still 1,030 feet higher than the world’s lowest spot, teh Dead Sea.

Death Valley is the driest place in North American. The relative humidity can be as low as 3%. The wettest month is February & the driest is June. However, the driest place in the world is Arica, a small village in the Atacama Desert of Chile.

Death Valley is definitely not a dead place. It is a dedicate and fragile intricacies complexity.
